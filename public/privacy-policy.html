Privacy Policy — East vs West

Last updated: 13 July 2026

East vs West (the “app”, package com.pisberg.eastvswest) is a single-player and local hotseat strategy game developed by Peter Isberg. This policy explains what the app does with your data. The short version: it does not collect any.

Data we collect

None. The app does not collect, transmit, sell or share any personal data. It has no user accounts, no sign-in, no analytics or crash-reporting SDK, no advertising, and no third-party trackers. It does not request the network, camera, microphone, location, contacts or storage permissions.

Data stored on your device

The game saves a small amount of gameplay state locally, using the standard browser localStorage mechanism inside the app. This never leaves your device and is not readable by us. It covers only:

  • Menu preferences — chosen side, map, win mode and CPU difficulty
  • Audio settings — mute, music toggle and volume
  • Display settings — effects quality and colourblind assist
  • Completed challenges and recent match results
  • Whether you have already seen the in-game hints

Uninstalling the app, or clearing its storage from Android’s app settings, permanently erases all of it.

Children

The app collects no data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If the app ever begins collecting data, this policy will be updated before that version ships, and the date above will change.
